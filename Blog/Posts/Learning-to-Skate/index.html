Learning to Skate

I have received many questions about how to learn to skate. I believe there are many different paths you could take to learn to skate and the way I learnt is not the only way. I do believe that there are main principles of learning that can be applied to any field. I’ll use this post to talk about the way I look at learning and relate it to my personal journey.

First of all, I don’t think the ‘how’ is the most important part. It does hold some importance but does not outweigh persistence and consistency. The hard part is not finding a way to learn especially now in the internet age with many tutorials freely available. The hard part is the consistent practising and persisting through your own self-doubts. This means the focus should be on doing something you enjoy and therefore being more motivated to push through the difficult moments. It is important to always take into account how far you have come during your pursuit of a skill and therefore enjoy each moment as it comes. It is good to look to the future and work towards something but be sure that you don’t get trapped in that hope and feel as if the final product will bring satisfaction. Enjoy the problem not the solution.

I believe to build a skill like skating, which needs a large amount of muscle memory, you need to practice frequently. When I was beginning, I practised every once in a while, for a large amount of time. I realised that I was not progressing very much. I then switched to practising for a short amount of time every day and the progress was substantially higher than I expected. I believe that practising 15 minutes every day is better that practising for 5 hours on once a week. Although the first option amounts to a lot less overall practice time I believe the repetition is what will lead to real progress.

Keep in mind this is only my experience and everyone learns differently. Don’t let anyone tell you that you are doing it wrong. I say this because the main thing I’ve learnt in life is no one really has the answers and that’s what makes it fun.
